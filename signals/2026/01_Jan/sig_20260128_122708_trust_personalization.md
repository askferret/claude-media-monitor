## Summary

Consumers want personalization on their terms: clear value exchange, control over data, and transparent privacy-first practices. Trust differentiates brands that earn attention, data, and loyalty; first-party data and identity resolution are framed as the foundation for that trust.

## Detailed Analysis

Experian and Human8 both emphasise that identity and self-expression through brands are contingent on trust. Experian: 64% prefer personalized experiences, but 39% say benefits outweigh privacy trade-offs and 32% feel uncomfortable with many data-driven tactics; first-party data is the foundation of meaningful, privacy-first engagement. Human8's 'recalibrating identities' theme implies people are more intentional about who they let into their identity constructionâ€”brands must earn the right to be part of that. Trust is not only data privacy: it includes transparent value, relevance without creepiness, and consistency across channels. For cultural analysts, the insight is that identity-based consumption is increasingly gated by trust; brands that fail on transparency or relevance are excluded from self-expressive choice.

## Implications

- Invest in privacy-first identity and clear value exchange; avoid opaque or overreaching personalization.
- Position brand as earning a place in self-expression through transparency and relevance, not volume of touchpoints.
